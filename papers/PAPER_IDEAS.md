### 4. Information-Theoretic Limits of p-Hacking: A Channel Capacity Bound on False Discovery

**Discipline:** Philosophy of Science / Information Theory / Statistics
**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High | **Acceptance:** High
**Journal Target:** Philosophy of Science

**Core Contribution:** Models the research pipeline as a noisy communication channel where the "signal" is the true effect and the "noise" is researcher degrees of freedom. Derives a channel capacity bound: given k researcher degrees of freedom, the maximum rate at which true discoveries can be reliably communicated is bounded above by a function of k. **Why the gap exists:** The replication crisis literature (Ioannidis 2005, Simmons et al. 2011) is empirical and simulation-based. No one has applied Shannon's channel capacity theorem to derive hard theoretical limits on what a corrupted research process can discover. The connection between information theory and philosophy of science is underexplored.

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High | **Acceptance:** Medium
**Journal Target:** Journal of Mathematical Sociology
