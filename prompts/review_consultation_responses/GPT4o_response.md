### PROPOSED NEW SCORED DIMENSIONS

1. **Methodological Soundness**
   - Evaluates the appropriateness and robustness of the research methods used.
   - Why it matters: Ensures reproducibility and validity of findings.

2. **Statistical Analysis**
   - Assesses the appropriateness and accuracy of statistical methods.
   - Why it matters: Critical for validating empirical results.

3. **Reproducibility**
   - Checks if the study can be independently replicated based on the provided information.
   - Why it matters: Core principle of scientific research.

4. **Argumentation Quality**
   - Evaluates the logical structuring and support of arguments throughout the paper.
   - Why it matters: Ensures persuasive and coherent presentation of ideas.

5. **Citation Integrity**
   - Checks for accuracy and appropriateness of citations.
   - Why it matters: Prevents misinformation and acknowledges rightful contributions.

### PROPOSED BOOLEAN CHECKS

1. **Citation Fabrication Detection**
   - Check for fabricated or non-existent citations.
   - Pass/Fail: Fail if any citation cannot be verified.

2. **Logical Fallacy Detection**
   - Identify logical inconsistencies or fallacies in arguments.
   - Pass/Fail: Fail if any major logical fallacies are present.

3. **Scope Creep Detection**
   - Ensure the paper remains within its defined research scope.
   - Pass/Fail: Fail if the paper deviates significantly from its stated objectives.

4. **Data Fabrication Signals**
   - Identify inconsistencies or red flags in data reporting.
   - Pass/Fail: Fail if data appears inconsistent or unverifiable.

5. **Methodology Appropriateness**
   - Check if the chosen methodology aligns with the research question.
   - Pass/Fail: Fail if the methodology is inappropriate for the study.

6. **Formatting Compliance**
   - Ensure adherence to journal-specific formatting guidelines.
   - Pass/Fail: Fail if formatting guidelines are not met.
