Pipeline improves rigor but dampens authorial voice. Single-pass Author produces bolder, more actionable output (Gemini ACCEPT, six 10s). Pipeline produces more defensible, methodologically tighter output (Gemini MINOR, Grok scores higher).

**Why:** The Peer Reviewer and Steelman force the Author to add caveats and qualify claims. This makes methodology tighter but makes prose more cautious. The user noticed this and questioned whether the pipeline is always necessary.

**How to apply:** Don't force everything through the full pipeline. For RWE reports and internal evidence, single-pass with external review is often sufficient. Reserve the full adversarial pipeline for regulatory submissions, client deliverables, and anything that needs to withstand hostile scrutiny. Offer both modes.
